OUR INSPIRATION

SHANTA BAI JI (September 29 1923-25th April 1935)


D/O Smt. Ratan Shastri and Pt. Hiralal Shastri
Our sweet lovely flower
withered before its hour.
Joy descended sorrow flew.
From the ashes garden grew.
ShantaBai is not a name to be cherished.
It is a conceptual symbol of
an educational and social philosophy
dynamic in approach,
rich in content ,
full of realism,
freshness and vitality.

Pt. HIRALAL SHASHTRI (24 November 1899 – 28 December 1974)


Co-Founder of Banasthali Vidyapith
The First Chief Minister of Rajasthan State in Northern India.
From early childhood he had a burning desire to go to some village and devote his life to the service of the downtrodden.He selected a remote and backward village Banasthali, 72 km from Jaipur and founded 'Jeevan Kutir' there. Here he trained a group of social workers and endeavoured to implement a programme of rural reconstruction. On 6 October 1935 Hiralal Shastri with his spouse founded Banasthali Vidyapith in memory of Shantabai .

RATAN SHASHTRI (5 October 1912 – 29 September 1988)


Founder Of Banasthali Vidyapith
Woman Freedom Fighter of Rajputana
Padma Shri in 1955, Padma Bhushan in 1975
Ratan Shastri converted to the simple and frugal lifestyle as she embraced Gandhian ideals. Despite, coming from an affluent family, she dressed in coarse khadi fabric and ensured that her children also wore Khadi.Inspired by the dream of her deceased daughter, Ratan Shastri started a school for girls on 6 October 1935. It was named Shri Shantabai shiksha Kutir in memory of her daughter. In its second year, the name was changed to ShriRajasthan Balika Vidyalaya.

Aditya Shastri (4 June 1963 – 24 May 2021)


Vice-Chancellor,Banasthali University
Titled as 'Aditya Bhaiya'
He was in the Governing Council of the Association of Indian Universities (AIU). He served for one year at the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR). Later he moved to Banasthali where he has remained until his death. He has authored five textbooks and published over 50 research papers. He has generated more than US$10 million in grants for Banasthali. He once said "Since it's inception Banasthali has evolved as unique institution and we should not only always remain wakefull about this uniqueness but should also remain devoted to maintain and cherish it."

ABOUT BANASTHALI VIDYAPITH

Banasthali Vidyapith is a prominent women's university located in Tonk, near Jaipur, in the state of Rajasthan, India. Established in 1935 by Smt. Ratan Shastri and Pandit Hiralal Shastri, the university has grown to become one of the largest residential universities for women in the world. The campus is set in a lush green environment, fostering a holistic and conducive atmosphere for education and personal development. Banasthali Vidyapith is known for its commitment to providing quality education and empowering women. The university offers a range of undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs across various disciplines, including arts, science, commerce, law, engineering, and management. The curriculum is designed to be comprehensive and in line with the latest developments in each field, ensuring that students receive a well-rounded education. One of the distinctive features of Banasthali Vidyapith is its emphasis on a harmonious blend of traditional values and modern education. The university encourages a holistic approach to learning, promoting not only academic excellence but also the overall development of an individual. This includes fostering a sense of social responsibility, leadership skills, and a commitment to ethical values.
The university's commitment to women's education is reflected in its efforts to provide a secure and supportive environment for female students. The residential campus ensures that students have access to a plethora of facilities, including well-equipped classrooms, laboratories, libraries, and sports facilities. The emphasis on holistic development is further evident in the extracurricular activities offered, ranging from cultural events and sports to social service initiatives. Banasthali Vidyapith has gained recognition for its research contributions and innovation. Faculty members and students engage in cutting-edge research across various disciplines, contributing to the academic community's knowledge base. The university also collaborates with industry partners and research institutions to enhance the practical applicability of its academic programs. The institution's commitment to inclusivity is reflected in its diverse student body, with students hailing from various parts of the country and even from different nations. This diversity contributes to a vibrant and multicultural learning environment, enriching the overall educational experience. Over the years, Banasthali Vidyapith has garnered accolades for its educational initiatives and has been consistently ranked among the top universities in India. The alumni of the university have made significant contributions to various fields, showcasing the impact of the institution on the professional landscape.
